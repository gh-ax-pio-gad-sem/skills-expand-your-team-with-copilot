# Activity Difficulty Levels

## Overview
Activities can now have an optional difficulty level to help students and teachers find activities appropriate for their skill level.

## Difficulty Levels

### Beginner
Activities marked as "Beginner" are suitable for students new to the activity or those with minimal prior experience.

**Color Code:** Green badge  
**Examples:** Chess Club, Programming Class

### Intermediate
Activities marked as "Intermediate" require some prior experience or familiarity with the activity.

**Color Code:** Orange badge  
**Examples:** Soccer Team, Weekend Robotics Workshop

### Advanced
Activities marked as "Advanced" are designed for experienced students who have mastered the fundamentals.

**Color Code:** Red badge  
**Examples:** Math Club, Sunday Chess Tournament

### All Levels (No Difficulty Specified)
Some activities don't specify a difficulty level, making them suitable for all skill levels.

**Badge:** No badge is displayed  
**Examples:** Morning Fitness, Basketball Team, Art Club

## Filtering

### Filter Options
- **Show All**: Displays all activities regardless of difficulty level
- **Beginner**: Shows only Beginner-level activities
- **Intermediate**: Shows only Intermediate-level activities
- **Advanced**: Shows only Advanced-level activities
- **All Levels**: Shows only activities with NO difficulty level specified

### How to Use
1. Navigate to the activities page
2. Look for the "Filter by difficulty:" section in the left sidebar
3. Click on any difficulty level button to filter activities
4. The filter works independently with other filters (category, day, time)

## For Teachers

When creating or updating activities, you can optionally specify a difficulty level. If no difficulty is specified, the activity will be considered suitable for all levels and will only appear when the "All Levels" filter is selected.
